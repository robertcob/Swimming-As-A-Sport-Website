Kings Of Competitive Swimming

Throughout the decades the majority of popular races in competitive Swimming have been dominated by only a select few individuals. The Likes of Michael Phelps, Ryan Locte and Chad Le Clos have made huge strives in shattering world records throughout the years. These titans are not only known for their success but also for controversial faults which have even caused the end of careers.

Michael Phelps

Michael Phelps or "The Baltimore Bullet" a currently retired American Competitive Swimmer is not only a huge name in the sport but also the most decorated Olympian of all time, with over 28 medlas in total.Phelps is the long course world record holder in the men's 100 meter butterfly, 200 meter butterfly, and 400 meter individual medley as well as the former long course world record holder in the 200 meter freestyle and 200 meter individual medley

Legal Issues

Although the perfect athlete, Phelpsfaced many problems in his competitive career.At age 19 in November 2004, Phelps was arrested for driving under the influence of alcohol in Salisbury, Maryland. He pleaded guilty to driving while impaired and was sentenced to serve 18 months' probation, photos of the man using a bong in february 2009 also surfaced damaging his image as an athlete. But regardless of these faults Phelps still ended his career with a bang in the Rio 2016 Olympics.

Ryan Lochte

Ryan Lockte is an American competitive swimmer and a 12-time Olympic medalist. He is the 2nd-most decorated swimmer in Olympic history, behind only Michael Phelps. His seven individual Olympic medals rank near the top in men's swimming. Suspension

Recently in 2018 Ryan Lochte has been suspended from competition for 14 months by the US Anti-Doping Agency after use of a prohibited substance. Yet the swimmer is determined to continue to train and to make a serious comback.

Chad Le Clos

Chad Le Clos is a South African competitive swimmer who is an Olympic, World and Commonwealth Games champion as well as the Commonwealth record holder in the 50 and 100-metre butterfly. He won gold in the 200-metre butterfly and silver in the 100-metre butterfly in the 2012 Olympics in London, and has also won silver medals in the 200-metre freestyle and 100-metre butterfly in the 2016 Summer Olympics. He is well known for being one of the very few swimmers to actually beat Michael Phelps in the olympic games.
