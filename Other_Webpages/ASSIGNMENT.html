About Each Stroke

Competitive swimming is one of the most popular olympic sports. With 4 main strokes,butterfly, backstroke, breastroke and freestyle. Within each stroke there are many varied distanced events including my personal favourite the 200m breastroke, one of the most difficult races in my opinion.

Butterfly

Butterfly is by far the most exciting stroke to see in competiton, but considered difficult to perform by many due to its excessive use of core strength. This is also the stroke dominated by Michael Phelps in international competitive swimmming.

Breastroke

Breastroke is the least popular of all the four strokes in the sport. It is very difficult to properly perform, currently there are roughly 30 men on the planet who have gone under the minute in long course 100m breastroke races. Adam Peaty the British olympian is currently No.1 in the world in the 100m Breastroke, and still continues to smash world records at major international competitons

Backstroke

Backstroke is a stroke known by many swimmers for its strenuousness in execution, the complexity of the start and turns make this a fearful stroke to race in, as so many aspects of the race can go wrong.

Freestyle

Freestyle is the most popular of the 4 strokes, and is by far among the most competitive with new names constantly taking new titles throughout international competitions.
